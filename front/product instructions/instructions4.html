Oxygen Welding Gas Cylinder Tank - Safe Operating Procedures

Reference the diagram above for proper valve and regulator connection before starting.

Section 1: Preparation and Inspection

  1. **Secure Cylinder:** Ensure the cylinder is **secured upright** to a wall or a suitable cart using chains or straps to prevent tipping.
  2. **Inspect Valve:** Check the cylinder valve opening for dirt or foreign matter. **Crack the valve** (open and close quickly) to clean the outlet before attaching the regulator.
  3. **Install Regulator:** Carefully attach the appropriate **oxygen regulator** to the cylinder valve outlet. Use the correct wrench and tighten securely.

Section 2: Operation and Pressure Check

  1. **Open Valve Slowly:** Stand to the **side** of the regulator and **slowly open the cylinder valve** (no more than one full turn) to pressurize the regulator.
  2. **Check Pressure:** Read the high-pressure gauge to confirm the cylinder pressure. The working pressure gauge should read zero until the torch valve is opened.
  3. **Set Working Pressure:** Adjust the regulator control knob to set the desired **working pressure** (consult your welding manual for correct PSI) on the low-pressure gauge.

Section 3: Shutdown and Storage

  1. **Close Cylinder Valve:** **Always close the main cylinder valve first** after use.
  2. **Bleed Pressure:** Open the torch valve or bleeder valve to release the gas from the regulator and hose lines. Wait until both regulator gauges read zero.
  3. **Relieve Regulator:** Turn the regulator adjustment knob counter-clockwise until it is loose.
  4. **Storage:** Replace the cylinder cap when the cylinder is not in use or is empty. Store away from flammable materials and heat sources.
**Critical Safety Note:** Oxygen intensifies combustion. **Never use oil, grease, or any petroleum product** on or near the cylinder, valve, or regulator, as this can cause violent combustion or explosion. Ensure adequate ventilation. This equipment should only be handled by **trained personnel**.
