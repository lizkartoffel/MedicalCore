CONTOUR®NEXT EZ Blood Glucose Monitoring System

Reference the diagram above for proper usage before starting.

Section 1: Setup and Power

  1. Check Power: Ensure the device has fresh batteries installed. The meter uses two standard AAA batteries.
  2. Prepare Device: Turn on the meter by inserting a test strip. The device will automatically power on and display a drop symbol indicating it's ready for testing.
  3. Wait for Ready Signal: The meter will perform a self-check and display the test strip symbol when ready to accept a blood sample.

Section 2: Patient Preparation

  1. Wash Hands: Clean hands thoroughly with warm water and soap. Dry completely to ensure accurate readings.
  2. Prepare Lancing Device: Load the lancing device with a sterile lancet and adjust the depth setting based on skin type.
  3. Select Test Site: Choose a fingertip (sides work best) or alternate site as approved by your healthcare provider.
  4. Insert Test Strip: Insert the test strip into the meter with the contact bars facing you. The meter will turn on automatically.

Section 3: Taking the Measurement

  1. Obtain Blood Sample: Use the lancing device to prick the side of your fingertip. Gently squeeze to form a small drop of blood.
  2. Apply Blood to Strip: Touch and hold the tip of the test strip to the blood drop. The meter will beep when enough blood is applied.
  3. Second-Chance Sampling: If needed within 60 seconds, you can apply more blood to the same test strip if the first sample was insufficient.
  4. Wait for Result: The meter will count down (usually 5 seconds) and then display your blood glucose result.
  5. Record Result: The meter automatically stores results with date and time. Write down the result in your logbook if required.

Section 4: Cleanup and Storage

  1. Dispose of Materials: Safely dispose of the used test strip and lancet in a sharps container.
  2. Clean Meter: Wipe the meter with a slightly damp cloth if needed. Never use alcohol or cleaning solutions on the meter.
  3. Store Properly: Keep the meter and test strips in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures.
Safety Note: This device is for self-monitoring of blood glucose. Always consult with your healthcare provider about your results and treatment plan. If you experience symptoms of low or high blood sugar, test immediately and follow your healthcare provider's instructions. Keep test strips in their original container and check expiration dates regularly.
